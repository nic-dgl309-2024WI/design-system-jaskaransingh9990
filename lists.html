Lists

Lists are fundamental structures in HTML used to organize and present information in a structured manner. There are three main types of lists in HTML:


1. Unordered Lists (<ul>): Unordered lists present items in a bulleted format. They are typically used when the order of items is not important.

2. Ordered Lists (<ol>): Ordered lists present items in a numbered format. They are used when the order of items is significant.

3. Definition Lists (<dl>): Definition lists are used to present terms and their definitions. They consist of a series of term-definition pairs.

How to plant?

  • Choose the right location with adequate sunlight for your plant.
  • Prepare the soil by loosening it and removing any weeds or debris.
  • Dig a hole slightly larger than the plant's root ball and gently place the plant into the hole.
  • Backfill the hole with soil, pressing gently to eliminate air pockets, and water thoroughly.
